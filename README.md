# Predicting Nutrient Loads in Urban Stormwater Runoff.

Urbanization has led to increases in impervious surfaces, altering hydrologic processes and contributing to increased levels of nutrients and sediment in stormwater runoff. Accurate prediction of Event Mean Concentrations (EMCs) is crucial for developing effective stormwater management practices and predicting pollutant load in receiving waters. This study evaluates the predictive performance of three machine learning models: Random Forest Regression, Gradient Boosted Regression and Bayesian Additive Regression Trees (BART). Data from the National Stormwater Quality Database was used to train the models and make predictions. The models were trained to predict EMCs for nitrate nitrogen, total phosphorus and total suspended solids.
Findings show that Random Forest Regression performed the best for nitrate nitrogen, with no improvement gained from gradient boosting or BART. For total phosphorus, both Random Fores and Gradient Boosted Regression outperform BART. BART performed the best for total suspended solids. The results show that no single model was optimal for all three targets. Overall, this comparative study shows that machine learning methods can effectively model nutrient and suspended sediment loads in runoff, though the optimal model varies by contaminant.
